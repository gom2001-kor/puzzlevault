How to Play MergeChain

MergeChain is a dynamic, physics-based puzzle where precision meets strategy. Your goal is to drop numbered balls into a vertical field, merging identical numbers to create larger ones and rack up massive scores.

Dropping and Merging: Touch and drag horizontally to position the next ball at the top of the screen. Release your finger or mouse to drop the ball. When two balls with the same number touch, they instantly merge into a single ball with double the value! A 2 touching a 2 becomes a 4; two 4s become an 8, and so on up to 2048 and beyond.

Chain Reactions: The real magic happens when merges trigger a chain reaction. If a newly merged ball bounces into another ball of its new value, they merge again! Building these combo chains multiplies your score significantly.

Watch the Danger Line: As your balls pile up, they will get closer to the top of the field. A dashed red Danger Line marks the upper limit. If any ball remains above this line for more than 3 seconds, the game is over! Keep the pile low by continuously creating efficient merges.

Game Modes: Play Classic mode to see how high you can score until the board fills up. Try Daily mode for a seeded sequence of drops that everyone in the world receives—perfect for comparing strategies. Or jump into Time Attack to score as many points as possible within a 2-minute limit.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do the physics affect gameplay?

Balls fall with gravity and bounce off the walls, the floor, and other balls. Larger numbers have larger radii, taking up more space and bouncing differently. You can use the bouncy physics to squeeze balls into tight gaps or create chaotic chain reactions!

How is the score calculated?

Every time two balls merge, you earn points equal to the value of the new, larger ball. If the merge triggers a chain reaction (a merge immediately following another), a chain multiplier is applied to the score of the subsequent merges.

What happens if a ball crosses the top line?

There is a danger line near the top of the screen. If a ball rests above this line, a 3-second countdown begins (indicated by a pulsing red border). Merging balls to bring the pile down will stop the timer, but if 3 seconds pass, it's Game Over.

What numbers will drop next?

The next ball to drop is always visible at the top of the screen. The dropped balls will randomly be either 2, 4, or 8, keeping the focus on merging them into larger numbers.
